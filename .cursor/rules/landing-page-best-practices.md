# 🏆 High-Converting Landing Page: Cursor Rules & Best Practices

## 1. Navigation Bar
- Simple, clear, accessible navigation.
- Logo, key links, and prominent CTA button.
- Semantic HTML and accessibility attributes.

## 2. Hero Section
- Strong, value-driven headline and subheadline.
- Social proof (e.g., "Trusted by 200+ Customers").
- Primary and secondary CTAs.
- Visual (image/video) representing the service/product.

## 3. Trust Signals
- Display logos of well-known clients/partners.
- Build credibility by association.

## 4. Services/Offerings
- 3–4 core services or features.
- Each with title, concise description, and CTA.
- Use cards or grid layout.

## 5. Process Section
- 3–4 simple, transparent steps.
- Icons, titles, and short descriptions.
- Reassure users of professionalism and clarity.

## 6. About/Company Section
- Brief intro to company, mission, or story.
- Key stats (clients served, projects completed, satisfaction rate, etc.).
- Humanizing image (team, founder, workspace).

## 7. Testimonials
- 2–3 authentic testimonials (video or text).
- Names, roles, and companies for credibility.

## 8. Why Choose Us/Comparison
- Checklist or comparison to highlight unique strengths.
- Make it easy to see why you stand out.

## 9. FAQ Section
- 3–5 common questions and clear answers.
- Accordion/expandable format for navigation.

## 10. Final CTA Section
- Repeat main offer and CTA.
- Support with a visual or testimonial.

## 11. Footer
- Company info, navigation links, contact, socials, legal links.
- Ensure accessibility and clarity.

---

## 🛠️ Implementation Guidelines
- Use TailwindCSS for styling; avoid custom CSS unless necessary.
- Componentize: Break sections into reusable, well-named React components.
- Accessibility: All interactive elements must be keyboard accessible and have ARIA labels.
- Early Returns: Use early returns in logic for clarity.
- Descriptive Naming: Use clear, descriptive names for variables, functions, and components.
- No Placeholders: Always provide complete, functional code—no TODOs or stubs.
- DRY Principle: Avoid code duplication; reuse components and logic.
- Responsive Design: Ensure layout works on all screen sizes.
- Minimal Prose: Keep copy concise and direct.

---

**Refer to these rules for every landing page project to ensure clarity, trust, accessibility, and conversion optimization.**
